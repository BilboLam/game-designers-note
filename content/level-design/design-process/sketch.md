# pencilSketch
Before you start building the level, try to draw a level diagram. This applies not only to complex 3D levels but also to 2D levels. When you feel you have a clear idea of the level, you can stop drawing the level diagram at any time and start making it.

Sketching for levels follows the steps below, similar to drawing.

Parti -> Bubble diagrams -> Floor plan## [hashtag](#id-1.-parti-thumbnails)1. [Parti thumbnailsarrow-up-right](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/layout#id-2.-parti-thumbnails)
"**Parti**" in architecture means the core concept of a project. In level design, it refers to the overall structure of a level.

parti diagrams from "101 Things I Learned in Architecture School" by Matthew Frederic**Don&#x27;t spend too long on each one.**

## [hashtag](#id-2.-bubble-diagrams)2. [Bubble Diagramsarrow-up-right](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/layout#id-3.-bubble-diagrams)
The goal of the **bubble diagram** is to establish proportions and relationships in your level.

example bubble diagram of F10 House in Chicago, from The Architecture Handbook: A Student Guide to Understanding Buildings by Masengarb et al.## [hashtag](#id-3.-floor-plan)3. [Floor Planarrow-up-right](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/layout#id-4.-floor-plan)
various floor plan drawing techniques from "Architectural Graphics" (6th ed.) by Francis D. K. ChingA **floor plan** is an imaginary horizontal slice through your level. Normally, we use bold lines for the level structure and represent [fillers](/game-designers-note/level-design/layout/level-components/level-elements) with thinner lines. [Floorplannerarrow-up-right](https://floorplanner.com/) is a great tool when levels go complicated.

After drawing the floor plan, try using background colors to represent different areas.

open world mission diagram with safe observation area in green and high security objective area in red, for Assassin&#x27;s Creed, by Phillippe Bergeron for GDC 2016: "Level Design Workshop: 360 Approach for Open World Mission Design"## [hashtag](#id-4.-fillers)4. [Fillers](/game-designers-note/level-design/layout/level-components/level-elements)
In this note, "**fillers**" refers to the game elements. Once the floor plan is finished, you can add fillers to create a clearer image of the entire level. However, don&#x27;t over-design in this phase, as detailed designs often change during build and testing, and too much content will make the level diagram clutter.
